National Parks FAQ

Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone was the world's first national park, established in 1872.
  • It is home to the largest concentration of geysers in the world, including the famous Old Faithful
  • Yellowstone is located primarily in the U.S. state of Wyoming but also extends into Montana and Idaho.
  • Yellowstone is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, bison, and elk. It's considered one of the last nearly intact ecosystems in the Earth's temperate zone.

Grand Canyon National Park:

  • The Grand Canyon is one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.
  • It is carved by the Colorado River and is over a mile deep and 277 miles long.
  • The park is home to diverse ecosystems, ranging from the Colorado River to the North Rim's alpine forests.
  • The Grand Canyon exposes rocks that are over 2 billion years old, providing a geological record that spans nearly half of Earth's history

Banff National Park:

  • Canada's first national park, Banff, was established in 1885 in the Rocky Mountains.
  • It features stunning mountain scenery, glaciers, and turquoise lakes, including Lake Louise and Moraine Lake.
  • Banff is part of the Canadian Rocky Mountain Parks UNESCO World Heritage Site./li>
  • The park has a network of over 1,000 miles (1,600 kilometers) of hiking trails, offering opportunities for visitors to explore the stunning landscapes on foot and encounter the park's diverse flora and fauna.
How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: More than 20,000
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site?

What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?
Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.
